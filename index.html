Synthetic Dataset Framework

Design Premise: This synthetic dataset instantiates the Proximity-to-Theory (P2T) framework without deriving from student work. Questions reflect pedagogical patterns observed across years of teaching critical literacy and theory engagement.

Distribution Pattern: 19% Core Engagement (10/54), 22% Engaged Analysis (12/54), 35% Tangential (19/54), 24% Conceptual Drift (13/54)

Adversarial Dimension: 16 questions (30%) demonstrate critical engagement styles, distinguished between unproductive critique (unfair framing, straw-manning) and generative critique (productive challenge, theoretical expansion).

The Four Proximity Zones

Core Engagement (10 questions): Direct analysis of sociotechnical imaginaries framework. Text-bound interrogation of definitions, evidence, mechanisms, and tensions within Dishon's argument.

Engaged Analysis (12 questions): Substantial engagement with expansion or critique. Extends framework with adjacent theory, operationalizes concepts, or offers text-consistent challenges.

Tangential (19 questions): Connected applications moving away from core argument. Educational translations, accessibility tasks, professional applications where theory recedes.

Conceptual Drift (13 questions): Significant departure using paper as launching pad. Broad forecasting, cross-domain jumps, or generic advice disconnected from textual analysis.
